Available Agents in the CIN Plugin

The CIN Plugin provides a powerful, two-part agent architecture designed for creating realistic and intelligent characters. This architecture separates the agent's decision-making 'brain' from its physical, animated 'body', allowing for maximum flexibility and power.

1. ACINUnifiedAgentBP: The Brain

ACINUnifiedAgentBP is the core decision-making entity of the plugin. It's a sophisticated, high-level agent that integrates multiple AI paradigms into a single, flexible controller. Think of this as the agent's mind.

Key Features:

  • Hybrid AI Behavior: Can switch between or blend different AI modes:
    • Utility-Based: Makes decisions based on the calculated utility of different goals.
    • Reactive: Responds immediately to environmental stimuli, like threats.
    • Predictive: Anticipates future events and plans accordingly.
    • Social: Manages relationships and interactions with other agents.
    • Learning: Adapts its behavior over time based on the outcomes of its actions.
  • Goal Management: Manages a list of available and active goals, driving the agent's long-term behavior.
  • Component-Based: Comes equipped with essential components like UCINBrain, UCINPerceptionComponent, and UCINCombatComponent.
  • Highly Configurable: Exposes dozens of properties to fine-tune every aspect of the agent's behavior, from reaction speed to social awareness.

2. ACINAnimatedAgentBase: The Body

ACINAnimatedAgentBase is a base class focused on the physical representation and animation of an agent. It provides the framework for bringing a character to life visually. It's designed to be driven by a higher-level controller like ACINUnifiedAgentBP.

Key Features:

  • State-Driven Animation System: Manages a map of animation states (e.g., Idle, Walking, Alarmed) and blends between them smoothly.
  • Dynamic Head & Eye Tracking: A built-in system to make the agent look at actors, specific locations, or in random directions to increase realism.
  • Standardized Locomotion Interface: Provides a clean interface (ICINLocomotionInterface) for external systems to request movement, rotation, and stops. This decouples the AI's intent from the animation implementation.
  • Gameplay Ability System (GAS) Ready: Implements IAbilitySystemInterface, making it ready for integration with Unreal's GAS.

How They Work Together

In a typical setup, you would create a Blueprint that inherits from ACINAnimatedAgentBase to set up your character's mesh and animations. You would then either add the ACINUnifiedAgentBP logic to it or have a separate ACINUnifiedAgentBP possess and control your animated character. The 'brain' (Unified Agent) makes a decision, such as 'investigate that sound', and sends a command to the 'body' (Animated Agent) through the locomotion interface to execute the movement and play the appropriate animation.
